EVALUATION (WASM / Frankenstein Fork)

- Strengths:
  • Future-proof: aligned with WASM’s rise as universal runtime.
  • Unique positioning: not another Unity/Unreal clone.
  • Modding-first: hot-reloadable modules attract community.
  • Safety: sandboxed execution reduces catastrophic crashes.

- Weaknesses:
  • Complexity: building both engine and runtime.
  • Performance: WASM boundary crossings add overhead.
  • Tooling: debugging, profiling, and ecosystem still immature.
  • Adoption: developers may resist unfamiliar workflow.

- Opportunities:
  • Attract “forbidden lamp” devs craving novelty and risk.
  • Build a platform, not just an engine.
  • Enable cross-platform ubiquity with minimal friction.
  • Position engine as the first serious WASM-native game framework.

- Threats:
  • WASM standards may shift, breaking assumptions.
  • Performance gaps may limit adoption for high-end games.
  • Ecosystem fragmentation (different runtimes, APIs).
  • Burnout risk: ambition may outpace execution.
