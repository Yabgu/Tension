Here’s a structured set of name suggestions, split into three categories to reflect different angles: **Functional** (clear and direct), **Philosophical** (reflecting the manifesto), and **Evocative** (more abstract but memorable). I’ve been critical about avoiding clichés and names that feel like marketing fluff.

---

### **Functional – Directly Reflecting Purpose**

These focus on the *architecture* and *systems-first* nature.

1. **Weld**

   * Suggests forging separate parts into a strong whole, perfectly fitting the idea of replaceable modules being fused at runtime.

2. **Spine**

   * Implies a core structure holding everything together, emphasizing the native core as the backbone of the system.

3. **Lattice**

   * Reflects a network of interconnected, swappable systems while being clean, minimal, and architectural.

4. **Forge**

   * A space for creating raw, dangerous, unbounded things; appeals to developers craving novelty and risk.

5. **Axle**

   * The central rotating component that other systems connect to, symbolizing the toolset as a hub rather than a monolith.

---

### **Philosophical – Manifesto-Driven**

These emphasize **authenticity, risk, and brutal honesty**.

6. **Bareframe**

   * Communicates minimalism and rejection of bloat, like a stripped-down skeleton ready to be built upon.

7. **Truthplay**

   * Suggests games built with radical transparency, reflecting the toolset’s honesty-first philosophy.

8. **Brutalwork**

   * A nod to “brutalism” in architecture and software design — raw, functional, and unpretentious.

9. **Sovereign**

   * Implies independence and full control, appealing to developers who want to escape corporate ecosystems.

---

### **Evocative – Abstract but Memorable**

These are more brand-like while staying grounded.

10. **Kairo** *(from Greek καιρός, meaning “the right moment”)*

    * Suggests precision and timeliness, ideal for a toolset built for the future and WASM-native development.

11. **Fracton**

    * Inspired by “fractals” and “fraction,” evoking modularity and endless complexity emerging from simple rules.

12. **Nexus** *(but **risky**, as it’s common)*

    * Represents a point of connection and interaction, aligning with the modular system concept — though it may be too generic.

13. **Zyra** *(invented word)*

    * Short, sharp, and unique while feeling technical and futuristic without becoming buzzword-y.

---

### **Top Recommendations**

* **Weld** – Simple, direct, and modular.
* **Lattice** – Best for conveying systems-driven architecture.
* **Brutalwork** – Bold and manifesto-driven, very distinct.
* **Fracton** – Evocative and fits procedural generation themes.
