Of course. This is a fascinating project with a clear and compelling philosophy. The naming challenge is to find a term that is both technically sound and ideologically resonant. Here is a list of original name suggestions, organized by category, that aim to meet your criteria.

Functional & Architectural
These names focus on the core technical structure of modular, interconnected systems.

Coupler

Why it fits: Directly describes the toolset's primary function: to couple a high-performance native core with a flexible WASM runtime, creating a cohesive whole from distinct parts.

Keystone

Why it fits: In architecture, the keystone is the central stone that locks all others in place. This name implies that your toolset is the critical, minimal component that enables a larger, stable structure (the game) to be built.

Substrate

Why it fits: A substrate is the base layer on which something else is built. This name positions your toolset as the fundamental, non-bloated foundation for unique creations, without imposing a specific structure on top.

Truss

Why it fits: A truss is a rigid framework made of connected parts. This evokes the minimalist, systems-driven architecture where each beam (module) has a clear purpose and can be designed or replaced independently.

Philosophical & Conceptual
These names reflect the project's manifesto of brutal honesty, systems-thinking, and platform-oriented design.

Praxis

Why it fits: Praxis means "the process by which a theory, concept, or skill is enacted, embodied, or realized." This name speaks directly to developers who value action and practical implementation over hand-holding and abstracted theory.

Brutalist

Why it fits: This boldly embraces the "brutal systems-first" design philosophy, drawing a parallel to the architectural style that celebrates raw materials and structural honesty over superficial design. It's an authentic, no-nonsense statement.

Verity

Why it fits: It means "a true principle or belief," emphasizing the project's commitment to honesty, authenticity, and rejecting the "hollow" or "PR-driven" nature of other tools. It’s a platform built on true, reliable systems.

Tesseract

Why it fits: A tesseract is a four-dimensional cube. The name suggests unbounded potential and a structure that exists beyond conventional (3D) limits, perfectly capturing the "unbounded creations" and future-proof, innovative nature of the toolset.

Evocative & Abstract
These names are more metaphorical but are chosen to evoke the feelings of innovation, connection, and raw potential.

Axiom

Why it fits: An axiom is a statement that is accepted as true, serving as the starting point for further reasoning. This frames your toolset as the fundamental, unquestionable truth upon which developers can build their unique logical worlds.

Loom

Why it fits: A loom is a tool for weaving separate threads into a cohesive fabric. This beautifully metaphors the act of connecting the native core (one thread) with the WASM gameplay logic (other threads) to create a final, intricate product.

Nexus

Why it fits: A nexus is a central or focal point where things connect. This name positions your toolset as the critical connection point between high-performance native code and dynamic, portable WebAssembly logic.

Recommendation for Final Selection
Before settling on a name, I strongly recommend you:

Check domain availability: Look for .com, .dev, and .io domains.

Search GitHub, package managers (e.g., npm, crates.io), and trademarks: Ensure the name isn't already used for a significant software library or tool.

Say it aloud: Does it sound good? Is it easy to remember and spell?

Based on your manifesto, names like Brutalist, Praxis, and Keystone seem particularly aligned with your desire for authenticity, systems-thinking, and a clear opposition to corporate rebranding fluff.
