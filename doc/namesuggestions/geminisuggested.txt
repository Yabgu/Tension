Functional Names
These names focus on the toolset's core purpose and design principles.

Nexus Framework: Suggests a central hub or point of connection for different systems, emphasizing the modular and interconnected nature of the toolset.

Wasm Forge: This name highlights the key technology, WebAssembly (WASM), and the idea of "forging" or crafting game systems, appealing to a hands-on, creator-focused audience.

Core Logic: A direct and honest name that positions the toolset as the essential, fundamental layer for building game logic, rejecting bloat in favor of pure functionality.

Architect Engine: This name conveys a focus on systems and structure, suggesting that the toolset is for developers who want to architect their games from the ground up, rather than using pre-packaged solutions.

Philosophical Names
These names lean into the project's manifesto, highlighting its philosophy of honesty and systems-first design.

Axiom Engine: An axiom is a self-evident truth, suggesting the toolset is built on fundamental, unshakeable principles—a perfect fit for a systems-first design.

Praxis: From the Greek word for "practice" or "action," this name represents the application of theoretical knowledge into practical, creative work, resonating with developers who want to build, not just configure.

Crucible Framework: A crucible is a severe test or a place where elements are forged together, reflecting the project's 'brutal systems-first design' and its appeal to developers who 'crave risk.'

Evocative Names
These names are more metaphorical and aim to spark curiosity and convey a sense of innovation and possibility.

Mesa: A mesa is a flat-topped hill with steep sides, symbolizing a stable, foundational platform for building something truly unique and elevated.

Canto: Meaning "to sing" or a section of a long poem, this name suggests that developers use the toolset to create their own unique, expressive "song" or story without limitations.

Tesseract: This name, from a four-dimensional cube, evokes a sense of complexity and boundless potential, hinting at the toolset's ability to enable truly "unbounded creations" while also being a nod to a complex mathematical object.
